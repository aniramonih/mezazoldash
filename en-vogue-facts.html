En Vogue: The R&B Legends Who Defined the ’90s

In the world of R&B, En Vogue stands tall as one of the most beloved girl groups of all time. With their soulful harmonies and powerhouse vocals, this quartet managed to strike a chord with music fans across the globe during their prime. Even to this day, their music remains iconic and continues to inspire generations of artists.

Singer’s Bio and Early Life

En Vogue was formed in Oakland, California, in 1989 by original members Cindy Herron, Terry Ellis, Dawn Robinson, and Maxine Jones. The group’s members were brought together by producers Denzil Foster and Thomas McElroy, who recognized the women’s collective talents while working on other projects.

Cindy Herron was born on September 26, 1961, in San Francisco, California. She began singing in church at a young age and later enrolled in Oakland’s Skyline High School, where she was a classmate of eventual bandmate Maxine Jones.

Terry Ellis, born on September 5, 1963, in Houston, Texas, joined a band while attending Prairie View A&M University. They performed covers of popular songs before eventually disbanding. Ellis then moved to Los Angeles, where she worked as a legal secretary by day and a singer by night.

Dawn Robinson, born on November 28, 1965, in New London, Connecticut, moved to California as a child. She began singing as a teenager and eventually joined a group called the Questions, which later changed their name to En Vogue after Robinson joined.

Maxine Jones, born on January 16, 1962, in Paterson, New Jersey, began singing at a young age, often performing alongside her mother. She moved to California in the 1980s and eventually met Cindy Herron while attending Skyline High School.

Age, Relationships, and Children

Today, Cindy Herron is 59 years old, Terry Ellis is 57, Dawn Robinson is 55, and Maxine Jones is 59. Each member has led a fascinating life both on and off stage.

In terms of relationships, Dawn Robinson was married to En Vogue’s former road manager, Dre Allen, and they had a child together. Cindy Herron married restaurateur Glenn Braggs in 1996, and they have two children. Terry Ellis has been married to Tony Johnson since 1995, and they also have two children. Maxine Jones married her longtime boyfriend, Derrick Jackson, in 2014.

Height

While their singing ability is what truly matters, it’s worth noting that the members of En Vogue range in height from 5’3″ to 5’9″. Cindy Herron is the shortest member at 5’3″, while Terry Ellis and Maxine Jones both stand at 5’7″. Dawn Robinson is the tallest member at 5’9″.

Career Beginnings and Breakthrough

En Vogue’s career began when they signed with Atlantic Records in 1989. Their debut album, “Born to Sing,” was released the following year and was an immediate success. The album’s lead single, “Hold On,” went to #2 on the Billboard Hot 100 chart and remained there for weeks.

The group’s follow-up albums, “Funky Divas” (1992) and “EV3” (1997), were equally successful. Songs like “My Lovin’ (You’re Never Gonna Get It),” “Free Your Mind,” and “Don’t Let Go (Love)” became instant classics and are still frequently played on the radio today.

Throughout their career, En Vogue has received numerous accolades. The group has won seven MTV Video Music Awards, three Soul Train Music Awards, and has been nominated for four Grammy Awards.

Top Songs

En Vogue has an extensive catalog of hits, but some of their most popular songs include:

– “Hold On”
– “My Lovin’ (You’re Never Gonna Get It)”
– “Free Your Mind”
– “Don’t Let Go (Love)”
– “Whatta Man” (featuring Salt-N-Pepa)
– “Giving Him Something He Can Feel”

These songs are beloved by fans and continue to be a staple of ’90s R&B music.

Net Worth

As of 2020, En Vogue collectively has a net worth of $50 million. The group’s success in the ’90s and their enduring legacy have made them a valuable and influential part of music history.

Legacy and Impact

En Vogue’s impact on music cannot be overstated. As one of the most popular girl groups of the ’90s, they paved the way for other female artists to follow in their footsteps and helped to redefine what R&B music could be.

Their music has inspired countless artists over the years, and their influence can be heard in the work of performers such as Destiny’s Child, TLC, and Xscape.

In addition to their musical accomplishments, En Vogue has been recognized for their humanitarian efforts. The group has worked with organizations such as the National Breast Cancer Foundation and the United Negro College Fund, using their platform to raise awareness and funds for important causes.

Conclusion

En Vogue’s music remains as timeless as ever, and their influence continues to resonate today. From their early beginnings to their chart-topping success, this group of powerhouse vocalists has carved out a special place in the history of R&B music. As they continue to tour and perform, fans can look forward to seeing En Vogue’s music live on for many years to come.
