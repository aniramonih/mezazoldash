Carrot Top Overview and Wiki

Early Beginnings and Rise to Fame

Scott Thompson, better known as Carrot Top, began his journey in Rockledge, Florida. Born on February 25, 1965, he grew up as the youngest son of a NASA engineer, a background that perhaps fueled his creative genius. His iconic red hair earned him the nickname Carrot Top during his school days.

After graduating from Cocoa High School in 1983, he pursued a degree in Marketing at Florida Atlantic University. It was here that Carrot Top first dipped his toes into the world of stand-up comedy, performing at open-mic nights and quickly becoming a campus favorite.

Early Breakthroughs

His big break came in 1991 when he performed on Comic Strip Live. This appearance paved the way for his performance on the television show Star Search, further elevating his profile. His unique style, characterized by the use of props, quickly made him a standout figure in the comedy scene.

Television and Film Appearances

He expanded his reach beyond stand-up comedy, appearing in various television shows and films. His notable TV appearances include Larry the Cable Guy’s Christmas Spectacular, Gene Simmons Family Jewels, Space Ghost Coast to Coast, Criss Angel Mindfreak, and Scrubs.

In film, he is known for roles in Chairman of the Board and other productions. Additionally, he lent his voice as a spokesman in commercials for 1-800-CALL-ATT and recorded a commentary track for The Rules of Attraction in 2002.

A Staple in Las Vegas

Since 2005, he has been a prominent figure in Las Vegas, headlining at MGM Resorts International properties. His residency at the Luxor Hotel in Las Vegas, which began on November 22, 2005, is particularly notable.

His shows are renowned for their energetic blend of comedy and music, featuring a plethora of props and his signature one-liner jokes.

Diverse Television Roles

Carrot Top’s versatility is evident in his wide range of television appearances. He was a guest judge on Last Comic Standing in 2008 and appeared on Don’t Forget the Lyrics! in 2010, assisting illusionists Penn & Teller.

His ability to roast celebrities was showcased in the Comedy Central Roast of Flavor Flav and Gene Simmons. He also made appearances in The Bad Girls Club, CSI: Crime Scene Investigation, Family Guy, Scrubs, and Robot Chicken.

Engagements Beyond Traditional Media

In addition to traditional media, he has made his mark on internet series and other platforms. He appeared on Between Two Ferns with Zach Galifianakis, Tom Green’s House Tonight, and The Joe Rogan Experience.
