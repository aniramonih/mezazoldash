Early Life and Career Beginnings

Buju Banton was born in Kingston, Jamaica, and grew up in a poor neighborhood known as Salt Lane. His love for music was evident from a young age, and he began performing under the moniker “Buju” as a teenager. Banton’s early work was heavily influenced by dancehall—a genre that emerged from reggae—and he quickly gained popularity in Jamaica with his gritty lyrics and unique sound.

Rise to Fame

The early 1990s marked a turning point in Banton’s career with the release of his album “Mr. Mention,” which became the best-selling album in Jamaican history at the time. His subsequent albums, including “Til Shiloh” and “Inna Heights,” solidified his reputation as a reggae icon and helped to increase his international following.
