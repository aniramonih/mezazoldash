Early Life

James Comey was born in 1960 on 14th December as James Brien Comey Jr. He was born to Joan Marie Comey together with J. Brien Comey. His birthplace was Yonkers, New York. He was born along with his three siblings, namely Peter, Christopher, and Patricia Comey.

The career of his father was in the line of the corporate real estate. On the other hand, his mother was not only a computer consultant but also a homemaker. His grandparent was an officer of the Yonkers Police Department before becoming a commissioner. In the early 1970s, they left Yonkers for Allendale, New Jersey.

Upon settling in their new home, he joined the Northern Highlands Regional High School. In 1982, he graduated from the College of William and Mary. His majors were chemistry as well as religion. Three years later, he earned Juris Doctor from the University of Chicago Law School.

In 1987, Comey and Patrice Failor tied the knot. The couple has six children comprising two sons and four daughters. Their names are Brien, Maurene, Claire, Collin, Abby, and Kate Comey.

Career

Post law school, James got an opportunity to serve as a law clerk for a United States District Judge. The man was John M. Walker Jr., who held that position in Manhattan. The next job was as an associate based in New York. He worked for Gibson, Dunn & Crutcher. Then, he began working at the U.S. Attorney’s Office. That is when he served at the Southern District of New York between 1987 and 1993.

During the tenure of President Clinton, the man was the Assistant U.S. Attorney from 1996 to 2001. For President George Bush, he was the United States Attorney for the Southern District of New York. He was the acting Attorney General as a result of the hospitalization of John Ashcroft.

He left the government to work in the private sector between 2005 and 2013. That is when he became the general counsel of Lockheed and then Bridgewater Associates. From 2013 to 2017, he was the FBI Director.
