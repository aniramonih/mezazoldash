Tulsa King 

Tulsa King is a crime drama TV series developed by Taylor Sheridan for Paramount+, with Terence Winter as the showrunner. The show features Sylvester Stallone, who takes on his first leading role in a scripted TV series, portraying a Mafia capo recently released from prison.

Is Tulsa King Based on a True Story?

Tulsa King is a work of fiction and not based on a true story. Taylor Sheridan, who created the show and is also known for his work on Yellowstone, has crafted a fictional tale for audiences to enjoy. While the series is entirely made up, producer David Glasser has noted that it draws inspiration from two distinct genres. In an interview with the Hollywood Reporter, Glasser lauds the show's clever blending of western and gangster elements.

Is Tulsa King a Real Story?

No, Tulsa King is not a real story. Although the series features Taylor Sheridan's signature style of violence and gritty realism, the story of Manfredi and the Invernizzis is entirely fictional. While the show incorporates common themes found in mob-related narratives, such as organized crime and power struggles, the characters and events are not based on real-life individuals or situations. Despite this, the creation of the show itself has an interesting backstory.
