Age, Biography and Wiki

Daniel Ash is an English musician, singer, songwriter, and record producer. He is best known as the guitarist and vocalist of the gothic rock band Bauhaus, and later for his work with Tones on Tail and Love and Rockets. Born on 31 July 1957 in Northampton, England, Ash is the son of a lorry driver and a nurse. He attended Northampton School for Boys, where he met future Bauhaus bandmate Peter Murphy. Ash began his musical career in the late 1970s, playing guitar in a punk band called The Craze. He then joined Bauhaus in 1978, and the band released their debut album, In the Flat Field, in 1980. Ash wrote and sang lead vocals on several of the band's songs, including "Bela Lugosi's Dead" and "She's in Parties". In 1983, Ash left Bauhaus and formed the band Tones on Tail with Bauhaus bassist David J. The band released two albums, Pop and Goodbye, before disbanding in 1984. In 1985, Ash and David J. formed the band Love and Rockets with former Bauhaus drummer Kevin Haskins. The band released seven studio albums and achieved mainstream success with the singles "So Alive" and "No New Tale to Tell". In the 1990s, Ash released two solo albums, Coming Down and Foolish Thing Desire. He also collaborated with other musicians, including Nine Inch Nails, The Cure, and Siouxsie and the Banshees. Daniel Gaston Ash (born 31 July 1957 in Northampton, England) is an English musician, songwriter and singer. He became prominent in the late 1970s as the guitarist for the iconic goth rock band Bauhaus, which spawned two related bands led by Ash: Tones on Tail and Love and Rockets. Recently, he reunited with bandmate Kevin Haskins to form Poptone, a retrospective of their respective careers, featuring Kevin's daughter Diva Dompe on bass. He has also recorded several solo albums. Several guitarists have listed Ash as an influence, including Dave Navarro of Jane's Addiction, Kim Thayil of Soundgarden and John Frusciante of the Red Hot Chili Peppers.
