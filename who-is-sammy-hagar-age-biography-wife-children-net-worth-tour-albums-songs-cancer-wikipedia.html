Biography

Samuel Roy Hagar (born October 13, 1947), widely recognized as the Red Rocker or Sammy Hagar, is a renowned musician celebrated for his prowess as a singer, songwriter, and guitarist.

Over over five decades, Sammy Hagar has showcased remarkable versatility, excelling in various genres and musical endeavors. Whether through solo ventures or collaborative efforts with different bands, his musical journey is a testament to diversity and enduring success.

Early Life & Education

Sammy Hagar, an incredibly talented musician, was born on October 13, 1947, in the vibrant city of Salinas, California. Throughout his formative years, Samuel was nurtured and supported by his loving parents, Gladys Hagar and Robert Hagar. 

Growing up in a close-knit family environment, he enjoyed the company of his three siblings: Robert Hagar, Velma Hagar, and Bobbi Hagar Harrell.

As he embarked on his educational journey, Sammy Hagar attended Fontana High School, where he undoubtedly honed his skills and passion for music. The invaluable experiences gained during this time would later contribute to shaping him into the accomplished musician he became.

Career

Sammy Hagar‘s musical journey traces back to the vibrant music scene of 1960s California, where he honed his craft with various bands. However, it was in 1973 that he found his breakthrough moment as a member of Montrose, the hard rock band led by Ronnie Montrose. Their debut album, Montrose, highlighted Hagar‘s exceptional vocal prowess and skilled guitar playing, earning critical acclaim and commercial success.

After departing from Montrose in 1975, Hagar embarked on a fruitful solo career, releasing numerous albums throughout the 1970s and 1980s. His signature blend of hard rock with pop sensibilities and memorable melodies resonated with audiences, with hits like I Can’t Drive 55 solidifying his place in the mainstream music scene.

Joining Van Halen as the lead vocalist in 1985 marked a new chapter for Sammy Hagar, propelling him to greater heights of success. The band’s collaboration resulted in hit albums like 5150, OU812, and For Unlawful Carnal Knowledge, revitalizing Van Halen’s sound and securing continued dominance in the music industry.

Hagar‘s career remained dynamic after parting ways with Van Halen in 1996. In 2008, he co-founded the supergroup Chickenfoot, achieving critical acclaim and commercial success. Simultaneously, Hagar continued to pursue his solo endeavors, releasing albums that showcased his musical versatility and enduring talent.

Beyond his music career, Sammy Hagar has made significant strides as an entrepreneur, notably by creating the renowned Cabo Wabo tequila brand and a chain of successful restaurants known for their vibrant atmosphere and delectable cuisine.

Personal Life

Sammy Hagar, aged 76, shared a significant marital bond with Betsy Berardi from 1968 to 1994. Together, they cherished the joys of parenthood, welcoming two children, Aaron Hagar and Samantha Hagar, into their lives.

Following their divorce in 1994, Hagar embarked on a new chapter of his life by marrying Kari Karte in 1995. With Kari, he once again embraced the journey of parenthood, celebrating the arrival of two more children, Kama Hagar and Andrew Hagar, into their loving family.

Previously, Sammy Hagar was married to Betsy Berardi from 1968 to 1994. Additionally, he had an encounter with Connie Hamzy.

Net Worth
