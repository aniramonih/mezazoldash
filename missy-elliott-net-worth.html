What is Missy Elliott’s Net Worth?

Missy Elliott, the American rapper, singer, songwriter, and music producer, has a net worth of $50 million. She was the first female rapper to be inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame and has sold more than 30 million records in the United States. Known for her innovative music videos and boundary-pushing style, Elliott is often hailed as a visionary artist.

Elliott rose to fame in the late 1990s with her debut album “Supa Dupa Fly,” which fused hip-hop and R&B and used futuristic aesthetics. Hits such as “Get Ur Freak On,” “Work It,” and “Lose Control” showcased her unique lyrical style and ability to create catchy, danceable tracks. She has received numerous accolades throughout her career, including five Grammy Awards. In 2019, she became the first female rapper inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame.

Aside from her solo work, Elliott has written and produced songs for various artists, including Aaliyah, Whitney Houston, and Beyoncé, further cementing her influence in the music industry.

Early Life and Education

Missy Elliott was born in Portsmouth, Virginia in 1971 to Patricia and Ronnie Elliott. Her family was active in church and she began singing in the choir at a young age. After experiencing domestic abuse, she and her mother relocated to the Hodges Ferry neighborhood of Portsmouth. Elliott graduated from Woodrow Wilson High School in 1990.

Missy Elliott’s Career Highlights

In 1991, Elliott formed the R&B group Fayze, which later became Sista. After the group disbanded, Elliott and producer Timbaland began working together and creating tracks for other artists. Elliott’s debut solo album, “Supa Dupa Fly,” was released in 1997 and was eventually certified Platinum. Her third album, “Miss E…So Addictive,” featured Ludacris and Trina and included the Grammy-winning single “Get Your Freak On.” Elliott has since produced hit singles for other artists, including Keyshia Cole, Jazmine Sullivan, and Monica. She has also collaborated with a wide variety of artists as a featuring vocalist on their tracks, such as The Pussycat Dolls, Demi Lovato, and Ariana Grande. Elliott’s most recent release is her 2019 EP, “Iconology.”

Television and Film Appearances

Missy Elliott has made numerous appearances on television shows, including “All That” (1997), “Family Matters” (1997), “The Wayans Bros.” (1998), “Party Monsters Cabo” (2009), and “What Chilli Wants” (2010). She also starred in her own reality television show “The Road to Stardom with Missy Elliott” in 2005.

Elliott has also provided vocals for the animated film “Shark Tale” (2004) and made appearances as herself in the films “Honey” (2003), “Fade to Black” (2004), and “Just for Kicks” (2005).

Missy Elliott’s Health Struggles

Missy Elliott is known for being very private about her personal life. However, in June 2011, she revealed that her absence from the music industry was due to medical issues. Elliott was diagnosed with Graves’ disease, a hyperthyroidism disorder, after nearly crashing a car due to leg spasms.
