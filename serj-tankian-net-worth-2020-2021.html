Early Life

Serj Tankian was born on the 21st of August, 1967 to Armenian parents in Beirut Lebanon. At the age of seven, he moved along with his parents to Los Angeles, California.

Tankian did his schooling in the Rose and Alex Pilibos Armenian School. After that, he was accepted to California State University, Northridge. It was during his college days that he began to learn musical instruments and write songs.

Tankian, on the 9th of June 2012 at a private ceremony, married his long-time Armenian girlfriend, Angela Madatayan. On the 24th of October 2014, he became a proud father with the arrival of their son Rumi.

He now resides in Warkworth, Auckland, New Zealand with his family. He is a strict Pescetarian.

Tankian has been an active participant and had helped organize many protests related to Armenian Genocide Recognition, Axis of Justice, PETA, etc. His songs, too address those burning topics and create awareness among people. They are, therefore, trying hard for the causes to be heard.

Career

Tankian’s first entry into the music was through the forming of the band “Soil” along with his classmates from school. Later on, Soil was changed to System of a Down, after a poem one of the bandmates, Malakian wrote called Victims of a Down.

Tankian’s first solo debut was with his record album “Elect the Dead” in the year 2007. The album has been released in three different versions under the label of Reprise Records. The albums that followed are “Imperfect Harmonies,” “Harakiri,” “Orca Symphony No. 1,” and “Jazz is Christ.”

His latest project Fuktronic is yet to be released. It is an electronic music collaboration with Jimmy Urine. And has collaborated with a handful of artists for various projects.

Other than his contributions to music, he acted in a few movies. These movies include “My Little Prince,” “The Last Inhabitant,” “1915” and “Furious,” a Russian historical fantasy film.
