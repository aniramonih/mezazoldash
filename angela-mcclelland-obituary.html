Remembering Angela McClelland: A Tribute to Her Life and Legacy

Posted by Billy Koelling on Monday, August 12, 2024

As her alleged vehicular homicide at the hands of her husband has left her community in a state of shock and grieving, Angela McClelland’s death serves as a reminder of the sad results of domestic abuse.

The terrible death of Angela Marie McClelland has rocked Fort Ripley to its very foundations.

Angela’s life was tragically ended on June 25, 2023, when her lifeless body was found close to the intersection of Legend Road and Killian Road.

Her family and friends are in a state of shock and bewilderment due to how abruptly and violently she passed away.

The Crow Wing County Sheriff’s Department launched an investigation into the circumstances surrounding Angela’s death when officials discovered that she had been killed by a vehicle after conducting an investigation.

On July 10, 2023, Tony James McClelland, her husband, was eventually arrested and charged with vehicular homicide.

Because of her generous spirit and outgoing personality, Angela was beloved by many people in her community, making the unexpected loss of her an especially difficult one.

Her loved ones are still struggling with the reality of her passing and are finding comfort in their memories of her full life.

Following Angela’s demise, the neighbourhood came together to support and stand in solidarity with her family and friends.

The community’s unrelenting support serves as a constant reminder that they will always be there for one another in times of need, even though nothing can fully fill the vacuum left by her departure.

Angela McClelland Obituary: Funeral Arrangements

In her obituary, Angela McClelland is remembered for her services as a devoted carer and cherished member of the Fort Ripley neighbourhood.

Her violent and unexpected passing serves as a reminder of how fleeting life is.

Along with her devoted pets Dixie and Snickers, Angela leaves behind a bereaved family that includes her sisters Desirae, Andrea, Alyssa, Amber, Ashley, and Alexis.

The family wishes that donations be made on Angela’s behalf to the Babinski Foundation rather than sending floral tributes.

On July 22, 2023, Nelson-Doran Funeral Home in Brainerd, Minnesota, will host a Celebration of Angie’s Life with a gathering of family and friends from 11 a.m. to 3 p.m.

Gilbert, Minnesota will host the burial at a later time.

Though Angela’s absence will definitely be felt keenly, those who had the honour of knowing her will treasure her memories always.

Who was Angela?

Angela, a close-knit family with two siblings, was the daughter of Patty and Jim Thompson.

She attended Little Falls High School and graduated before deciding to work as an elderly carer. She was adored by her clientele and well-known for her compassion and kindness.

After dating for more than 20 years, Fort Ripley native Angela married Tony James McClelland.

At the time of Angela’s passing, they shared four kids while living together.

Angela is remembered by friends and family as a loving mother and wife who was always there for her family. She cherished spending time with her loved ones, going fishing, and camping.
