Danny Green

Daniel Richard Green, an American professional basketball player, formerly played for the Cleveland Cavaliers in the NBA. He had a remarkable college basketball career at the University of North Carolina (UNC), surpassing previous records with 145 games played and 123 wins, the highest among Tar Heels. Green holds a unique distinction in the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC) as the only player to achieve at least 1,000 points, 500 rebounds, 250 assists, 150 three-pointers, 150 blocks, and 150 steals.

In 2009, Green earned an NCAA championship during his senior year and was subsequently drafted by the Cleveland Cavaliers as the 46th overall pick in the 2009 NBA draft. His performance in the 2013 NBA Finals set a record for the most three-point field goals made in a Finals series with 23. The following season, Green won an NBA championship with the San Antonio Spurs, joining the elite company of James Worthy and Michael Jordan as the third UNC player to win both an NCAA championship and an NBA championship. He continued his success by winning another NBA championship in 2019 with the Toronto Raptors, followed by a third championship in 2020 with the Los Angeles Lakers.

What is NBA?

The National Basketball Association (NBA) was established in 1946 and stands as a premier professional basketball league in North America. Renowned worldwide, it consists of 30 teams divided into two conferences and six divisions. Widely recognized as the top basketball league globally, the NBA showcases an array of exceptional players who have achieved immense popularity and garnered enormous fan bases.
