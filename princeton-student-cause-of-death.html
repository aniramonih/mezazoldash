Princeton Student Cause Of Death Was It A Suicide Or Murder?

Posted by Patria Henriques on Monday, August 26, 2024

After a Princeton student was found dead after going missing, people wanted to know the cause of death.

On Wednesday, the local authorities released their confirmation that the Princeton student that went missing from the campus in October one week was found dead. 

The local authorities and the Princeton school confirmed the identification of the student. The student that went missing was Misrach Ewunetie, who was 20 years old at the time she went missing. 

According to sources, it was confirmed that on October 20, the Princeton student, Misrach, was found after missing for over six days. The place where her dead body was found was the ground behind the tennis court.  

She was last sighted alive after a live music event at the school’s Terrace Club. The club’s leader Alexander Moravcsik reported that Misrach volunteered in housekeeping tasks to help out others. 

Misrach’s family contacted the school after they didn’t hear anything from their daughter for two days. Misrach was from Ethiopia, but she resided in Euclid, Ohio, with her family. Living there, she majored in sociology and then went to attend her university. 

Her brother told a strong source that he saw her sister in her dorm room on campus around 3 AM on October 14. He said his sister was a “gem” to their family. 

W. Rochelle Calhoun, the school’s Vice President for Campus Life, came out to address that Misrach’s death was an “unthinkable tragedy” after her dead body was discovered. 

She worked as a business analyst at Bank of America as an intern and at McKinsey & Company in Ohio for two summers.

Her LinkedIn profile verifies it. It was known that the ambitious student was studying in an Ivy League school after earning a full scholarship.

Misrach Ewunetie – Princeton Student cause of death

The Middlesex Regional Medical Examiner conducted her autopsy to know the cause of her death. After all the tests were performed, the medical examiner released her autopsy, ruling her death to be a suicide. 

Mercer County Prosecutor’s Office released a statement in which it was said, “The cause of her death was determined to be bupropion, escitalopram, and hydroxyzine toxicity.”

Hydroxyzine – Medication

It is an antihistamine that is prescribed for the patient having the following problems:

  • Allergies
  • Anxiety

Bupropion – Medication 

It is a type of medication that is prescribed for patients dealing with depression. 

Escitalopram – Medication

This type of medication is similar to Bupropion, and it is also prescribed for patients dealing with depression.
