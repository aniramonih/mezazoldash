Hexagram 60 iChing: Limitation - I Ching 60 Video

Posted by Patria Henriques on Thursday, August 22, 2024

What Is I Ching Hexagram 60 All About?

Chieh is the Chinese name of the I Ching Hexagram 60. This has inner and outer trigrams forming water over the lake. People call this ‘limitation’ or ‘moderation’ or ‘articulating.’

Hexagram 60 gives wisdom on the importance of moderation. Our abilities might be limitless, but our capability to multitask and take on extra work has its limits. There is a common saying that ‘there is not enough sunlight in one day’. This is based on all the aspects of life that are constantly calling for our attention.

I Ching Hexagram 60 Interpretation and Meaning

As humans, we are always striving to accomplish more. In one single day, we attempt to do many different tasks to feel accomplished. And, also to prepare for the next day of tasks. Hexagram 60 gives wisdom on the importance of moderation.

Setting limits to our abilities is important. We think we can take on extra tasks and responsibilities. But, this may destroy the possibilities of success when we cannot focus on the progress.

It is straightforward to become overwhelmed with daily life demands. We have our responsibilities, but then we are responsible for the well-being of others. We cannot just focus on ourselves. But, we fill our minds with the events in others’ lives.

This pertains mostly to our children. We become so focused on our children’s progressions that we often forget about our own. It takes a whole village to raise a child. But, it only takes a moment to realize we are doing too much for others and not for ourselves.

This is not to say that we should neglect our children for our benefit. It simply means that we need to find balance and harmony in the home life.

I Ching Hexagram 60 Goals – Personal Goals

I Ching 60 says you should be willing to set limitations in all aspects of life. There is no possible way to take on the world and complete all the tasks that are set up for you. Rushing through life will only cause a pile-up of work and responsibility that then makes it difficult to progress.

It is better to produce a high-quality product than to have a high quantity of poorly made stuff. Take the time to examine your life. See what could be cut out to nourish other areas of life.

Getting rid of tasks that bring no joy or happiness should be discarded first. If you’re only working to appease others and not yourself, you will not be able to gain any success.

I Ching Hexagram 60 Love – Love Interpretation

Friends and family members often ask for favors that can be unexpected and during a difficult time. We must be willing, to be honest with ourselves. And, also with those we surround ourselves with when it comes to our limitations. Saying ‘no’ to a situation does not mean you will miss an opportunity. But it will ensure progress in the areas you need it most.

I Ching Hexagram Number 60 – Career and Business

I Ching 60 says that we are very willing to succeed and put in the time and effort needed to gain great accomplishments. But we may seem too eager to take on a lot of extra work. This could be an additional project at work on top of the one that is already in progress. There could be an organization that asks for our help that is outside of work. Now, we have two different areas in our lives that are asking for our time.

iChing 60 Judgment – The Judgment Poem

LIMITATION. Success.
Galling limitation must not be persevered in.

iChing 60 Poem – The Image Poem

Water over the lake: the image of LIMITATION.
Thus the superior man
Creates numbers and measures,
And examines the nature of virtue and correct conduct.

iChing 60 – Conclusion

Hexagram 60 is full of wisdom when it comes to the quality of life. Human beings are capable of accomplishing great things. But you cannot do this when life is full of multiple tasks.

When you distract yourself from what matters most in life, you waste your effort. You spend time on things that will have no benefits in the end. Make sure to set limitations on what you can handle. Be honest with yourselves and those you interact with.
