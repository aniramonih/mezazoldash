Juliette Pochin Full Overview and Wiki

Early Career and Breakthrough

Juliette Pochin‘s career took a significant leap forward in 2005 when she was signed by Sony BMG to record her debut album, Venezia, which was released in 2006. This album not only showcased her versatile vocal talent but also earned the title of Classic FM’s Record of the Week.

The release of Venezia was a defining moment in Juliette’s career, establishing her as a crossover artist capable of bridging the gap between classical and popular music genres.

Crossover Success and Collaborations

Juliette’s ability to blend classical music with crossover elements has led to unique collaborations and projects. Notably, her voice inspired a new chocolate range by Hotel Chocolat, named after her following a description of her voice as sounding like melted chocolate in The Times. Additionally, she has been a soloist at the BBC Proms in the Park from Salford, demonstrating her appeal to both classical and broader audiences.

Morgan Pochin Music Productions

Alongside her husband, James Morgan, Juliette formed Morgan Pochin Music Productions Ltd., a venture that has seen the duo produce records for renowned artists such as Katherine Jenkins and Alfie Boe.

This production company has become synonymous with high-quality recordings and arrangements for film and television scores, further cementing Juliette’s reputation in the music production industry.

Recent Projects and Performances

In recent years, Juliette has continued to expand her repertoire and influence. She has been a regular orchestrator and musical director for BBC Radio 2’s Piano Room and composed the music for Let There Be Peace, a poem by Lemn Sissay, for the Brighton Festival.

Juliette also ventured into the world of musical cabaret, creating Music, Mayhem and a Mezzo during the lockdown, a show that has enjoyed success at various prestigious venues, including The Crazy Coqs and The Pheasantry, and at the Edinburgh Fringe Festival.

Contribution to Music and Media

Juliette’s contributions extend beyond her performances and recordings. With James Morgan, she has produced music for films, including adapting Mozart’s music for the British film First Night and producing the music for Dustin Hoffman’s directorial debut, Quartet.

Their work on Light for the World with the Poor Clares of Arundel debuted at No. 1 in the UK Classical music chart, showcasing their ability to create impactful and well-received musical projects.

FAQs about Juliette Pochin

Who is Juliette Pochin?

She is a Welsh classically trained mezzo-soprano singer, composer/arranger, and record producer, known for her performances in operas and as a classical recitalist as well as her recordings of operatically styled crossover music.

What is her educational background?

She studied music at Cambridge, where she was a choral scholar, and later at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama.

Has she collaborated with other musicians or composers?

Yes, she has worked with artists ranging from Alfie Boe to Pete Townshend and Katherine Jenkins, and composed and arranged music for television shows like The Kumars at No. 42.

What are some of her notable performances and compositions?

She has performed internationally, given world premieres of compositions such as Sofia Gubaidulina’s Canticle of the Sun, and worked on film and TV music, including scores for First Night and Quartet.

What are some highlights from her discography?

Her notable recordings include Venezia, a solo album that was Classic FM’s Record Of The Week, and The Sky Shall Be Our Roof, featuring rare songs from the operas of Ralph Vaughan Williams.

Does she engage in charity work?

She, alongside James Morgan, has raised significant funds for Parkinson’s UK through their Symfunny concerts at the Royal Albert Hall.

What is Morgan Pochin Music Productions?

It is a company formed by her and James Morgan, specializing in music production, composition, and arranging, with a portfolio that includes working on several albums and film scores.

Has she produced any albums?

Yes, as a record producer, she has worked with major labels, producing albums for artists such as Alfie Boe, Katherine Jenkins, and Sir Cliff Richard, among others.

Is she involved in music education?

She is passionate about music education, particularly in encouraging young people to sing in choirs. She co-founded the Brighton Festival Youth Choir.

What are her recent or upcoming performances?

She continues to perform and provide music at various venues, including the Crazy Coqs in London and the Edinburgh Fringe.
