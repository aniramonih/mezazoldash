Early Life & Biography

Jinder Mahal was born on 19th July 1986 in Calgary, Alberta, in Canada. He was inspired by his career since he was young because his uncle, Gama Singh, was also a professional wrestler. His uncle was so popular around the 1980s and was a legend to the Stampede Wrestling and later joined World Wrestling Federation (WWF), which later changed its name to WWE. He has an Indian origin and can speak Punjabi, Hindi, and also English. He attended the University of Calgary, where he graduated with a degree in communications and culture.

Career

Mahal began his career early in his life when he began to be trained by Rick Bognar at Martial Arts Fitness Center. He did his first match at Premier Martial Arts Wrestling (PMW), where he took the name of Raj Dhesi. He was training together with Allen Coage, Gerry Morrow, Natalya, Tyson Kidd, and Viktor. He joined World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE) in 2010, where he took the ring name, Jinder Mahal. He joined the WWE SmackDown episode on April 29, 2011. He was together with Great Khali and Ranjin Singh.

He teamed up with Drew Mclntyre and Heather Slater after him, and Mclntyre interfered with Slater’s match by attacking Brodus Clay, who was wrestling with Slater. They formed their alliance called 3MB, which was able to record many wins together in the matches that followed. Mahal was withdrawn from the WWE contract in June 2014.

He joined Reality of Wrestling (ROW), where he took the name, Raj Singh. Here he also teamed up with his cousin, Gama Singh Jr., to win in All-Star Wrestling (ASW). He also participated in other teams as World Wrestling Council (WWC), Qatar Pro Wrestling (QPW), and Continental Wrestling Entertainment (CWE).

Mahal joined back WWE in July and began making peace with fellow wrestlers in WWE, saying that he was angered when he left WWE and had found peace after joining back. Since then, he was part of WWE and has participated in a number of matches but involved in a knee injury in June 2019, which was said to take him about 6 to 12 months for him to regain recovery. He joined back on 27th April 2020, where he participated in RAW and defeated Akira Tozawa.

Awards & Achievements

In 2017 he won the WWE Championship, which made him the 50th WWE champion and also the first Canadian to have that position. He has won many more titles in his career, including the most improved wrestler of the year 2017 and most hated wrestler still in 2017.
