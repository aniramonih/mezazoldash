Ally Shipman is a successful and well-known softball player who began playing softball professionally in the year 2019. She is the driving force behind the success of Alabama Crimson Tide Softball throughout her entire career. 

Ally made her collegiate debut on February 8 against UIC, hitting a home run and collecting three RBIs. She also had two runs, a home run, and an RBI against New Mexico. In 2019, she was named to the SEC’s First-Year Academic Honor Roll. In the victory over South Carolina and the victory over Kentucky, Ally went 2-for-3 and tallied three RBIs.

Early Life

Ally Shipman, the daughter of Bill and Lorri Shipman, was born on October 25, 1999. She has one brother, Billy, and two sisters, Amanda and Madison. From 2011 until 2014, Madison, her sister, played softball for Tennessee. She has majored in Human Environmental Sciences.

Ally studied at Valencia High School in California’s Valencia. She finished high school with a 4.2 GPA. Ally Shipman was recognized as a member of the Extra Innings Softball 2018 Elite 100 and a 2018 PGF Regional All-American. She also won the 2017 Foothill League Player of the Year award. With seven home runs, five triples, 34 walks, 25 RBI, and a.528 on-base percentage as a senior, she batted.356.
