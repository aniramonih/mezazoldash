The 12 Best Drugstore Waterproof Mascaras of 2024by InStyle

Posted by Tandra Barner on Monday, August 12, 2024

For sob-worthy wedding ceremonies, sweat-inducing heat waves, and beyond, the best drugstore waterproof mascaras can prove essential for keeping your eye makeup from running. The appeal of these formulas isn’t just that they’re accessible, often running in the single- or low double-digits per tube. They also offer budge-proof wear come rain or tears, resisting streaking and smudging where other, traditional formulas can’t.

Naturally, that’s made them a favorite among makeup artists and savvy customers alike. However, no waterproof formula is exactly the same, so it’s important to have an idea of what to look for. Want to go full-blown glam? You’ll need one that can thicken lashes without leaving them stiff or brittle. Into the no makeup-makeup look? Then your best bet is a mascara that separates and conditions lashes without clumping or smudging. No matter the makeup look you’re going for, you can achieve the volume, curl, length and staying power you want with a stroll down the drugstore makeup aisle.

Best Overall

L'Oréal Paris Voluminous Waterproof Mascara

Amazon

What We Love: It leaves lashes feeling soft and touchable.

What We Don’t Love: The formula doesn’t offer much by way of length.

This mascara from L'Oréal is a longstanding favorite of makeup artists across the world, and for good reason. “I've used it for years,” says makeup artist Lauren O’Leary. “It's very black, buildable for volume, and keeps the lashes curled.” Meanwhile, celebrity makeup artist Andréa Tiller calls it a cult-favorite for similar reasons. “It's super-black and lightweight but effective — which means it keeps your lashes up and curled!”

In other words, it checks all the boxes most of us want out of mascara. And, in addition to curling and volumizing, it separates every lash to minimize clumps, even if you apply multiple coats (which you should, since the volume is buildable). It also stays put without leaving lashes feeling brittle or crunchy, imparting a full-on flutter that’s tough to beat.

Shades: 2 | Size: 0.28 fl oz

Best Budget

It's also infused with vitamin E to actively moisturize and protect lashes — which is key if you're applying (and removing) waterproof mascara on a regular basis. It's also sweat-, humidity-, and smudge-resistant, in addition to being waterproof. 

Because it leaves lashes feeling soft and flexible, you can reapply more throughout the day or before a big night out. Keep in mind, however, that the formula can take some time to set, during which the mascara can smudge around the eyes. We recommend waiting one full minute before adding another layer.

What to Keep in Mind

Formula

Waterproof mascaras can offer various features, so thinking about not only the type of eye look you plan to create regularly, but also whether your eyes lean sensitive, watery or oily, will help you narrow down your options. For those who have sensitive eyes, for instance, Tiller seeks out fragrance-free formulas, since they tend to be hypoallergenic. “I also look at the benefits and features, like volumizing, lengthening, and most importantly, lifting,” she says, while anyone with oily lids should swipe on a tacky, mattifying eyeshadow primer in a skin-matching shade before layering on mascara.

Meanwhile, Scibelli prioritizes products that are true to their word. “Look for formulas that don’t flake or smudge throughout the day,” he says, adding that it’s “always a plus when there are lash-savvy ingredients as well for conditioning and hydrating.”

Brush

The brush can seem like an afterthought, but it can actually make or break the mascara and how it performs. To that end, “the brush is everything,” says Tiller. She prefers those with harder, stiffer bristles, as they tend to reliably deliver “clump-free results and separation, which is most important for me.”

The size of the brush matters, too. While a fat brush might seem satisfying and efficient, getting a thick coat of mascara with just one swipe — and it can be, in the right hands — “a skinnier brush is going to be ideal for separating and lengthening,” says Scibelli. “I also love using a smaller, skinny brush on the lower lashes for detail.”

Finish

First, consider what you want your lashes to look like. “Shop your personal concern for your lashes — do you need more volume? More curl or separating? More conditioning and hydrating?” Scibelli says. “Once you know what you’re looking for, you can then seek out what the various mascaras offer.”

If you’re looking for a drugstore mascara that stays put — makes sense, if you’re seeking a waterproof mascara in the first place — you may want a formula that dries down quickly. “They're easy to use, and the formula is buildable for volume but stays in place,” says O’Leary.

Your Questions, Answered

What's the difference between waterproof and smudge-proof?

It seems like a matter of semantics, but there’s a definite difference between waterproof and smudge-proof. “Waterproof does mean smudge-proof, but smudge-proof does not necessarily mean waterproof,” says cosmetic chemist Ginger King.

The term “waterproof” implies the formula avoids smudging due to swimming, rain, or crying. Meanwhile, she says, “smudge-proof is to prevent smearing of mascara due to interaction with oily foundation or rubbing.”

Is it okay to wear waterproof mascara every day?

Yes, it’s perfectly safe, says King. If you have sensitive eyes, you might want to consider a hypoallergenic formula, but it’s not always necessary.

How do you remove waterproof mascara?

King recommends using an oil-based cleanser, such as a cleansing balm or cleansing oil. For reasons that make complete sense, “a water-based cleanser won't do because the mascara is ‘water-proof’,” she says.

How do you keep mascara from smudging under your eyes?

It’s all about the angle. While you can apply your mascara as usual on the upper lashes — by wiggling the brush at the base of the lashes and sweeping it up — the lower lashes are a little trickier.

“When applying, instead of holding the wand horizontally, hold the wand in a vertical direction with just the tip of the wand touching your lashes as you apply the product,” says Tiller. “Move back and forth to distribute evenly.” That way, it delivers product without touching skin.
