Durga Prasai: A Multifaceted Nepalese Figure

Durga Prasai is a prominent figure in Nepal, known for his diverse roles as a businessman, politician, and medical entrepreneur. With a wealth of experience and accomplishments, he has made a significant impact in various fields.

Background and Education

Prasai hails from a prosperous business family in Jhapa, Nepal. Although he did not complete his formal education, his entrepreneurial spirit led him to join his family business at a young age. Despite facing challenges along the way, he persevered and gained valuable experience as a trader and contractor.

Establishment of B & C Medical College and Hospital

Driven by a vision to provide top-notch medical services, Prasai founded the esteemed B & C Medical College and Hospital in 2012. Situated in the bustling commercial hub of Birtamode, the hospital boasts a capacity of 750 beds and offers a wide range of medical courses and degrees. With affiliations to prestigious institutions and recognition from regulatory bodies, the hospital has become a beacon of excellence in the healthcare sector.

Involvement in Business and Other Ventures

Prasai’s entrepreneurial ventures extend beyond the medical field. He has diversified his interests into real estate, agriculture, and tourism, owning properties and hotels both in Nepal and abroad. Additionally, he has made significant contributions to the hydropower and aviation sectors, solidifying his position as one of Nepal’s most influential and affluent individuals.

Prasai’s involvement in politics cannot be overlooked either. As a member of the CPN-UML, Nepal’s largest political party, he has played an active role in supporting the party’s policies and decisions. His close association with former Prime Minister KP Sharma Oli has further cemented his influence within the political landscape.

Despite his achievements, Prasai has not been immune to controversy. His controversial statements and actions have sparked public outrage and criticism. However, his dedication to serving the nation and contributing to Nepal’s development and prosperity remains unwavering.

Membership in CPN-UML

Prasai’s Campaign and Demands

Restoration of Monarchy and Hindu State

At the forefront of Durga Prasai’s campaign is the call for the restoration of the monarchy and the reestablishment of Nepal as a Hindu state. Prasai argues that the current federal republic system has failed to deliver good governance, development, and national unity. He believes that reinstating the monarchy and embracing Nepal’s Hindu identity will bring stability and preserve the country’s cultural heritage.

Abolition of Federalism and Proportional Representation

Prasai advocates for the abolition of federalism and proportional representation, which were introduced in the 2015 constitution to address the grievances of marginalized and ethnic groups. He argues that these provisions have created divisions and conflicts among the people, weakening the sovereignty and integrity of the nation. Prasai also opposes the secularism enshrined in the constitution, calling for the protection of Hindu culture and values.

Loan Waiver and Economic Relief

In response to the economic crisis caused by the COVID-19 pandemic and government mismanagement, Prasai demands a waiver of bank loans up to Rs 2 million, availability of loans at a low interest rate, and restructuring of loans with a longer installment period. He believes that these measures are necessary to provide relief to the people who are suffering and to alleviate the burden of debt on individuals and businesses.

Resignation of Prime Minister and Fresh Elections

Prasai calls for the resignation of Prime Minister Sher Bahadur Deuba and the dissolution of the parliament. He believes that the current government, formed after the Supreme Court’s verdict in July 2021, is illegitimate and incompetent. Prasai alleges that external forces influenced the court’s decision and that fresh elections are needed to elect a new government that can fulfill the aspirations of the people.

Prasai’s campaign has garnered significant attention and support, mobilizing thousands of supporters across the country. While his demands may be controversial and subject to debate, they reflect his vision for a Nepal that he believes will be more prosperous, united, and aligned with its cultural roots.
