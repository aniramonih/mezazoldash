Career

O’Leary first worked as an assistant brand manager in 1979 at Nabisco’s cat food brand then co-founded Special Event Television and dealt with the production of sports, documentaries, and minor television shows. He later sold his share of the company.

With the money, he got from the sale of SET he went to Toronto and started Softkey in his basement. Softkey company used to deal with publishing and distribution of CD-ROM based software’s. The company in 1990 enjoyed prosperity as it dealt with educational software products.

With growing profits, Softkey purchased The Learning Company (TLC) for $600 million in 1995. Over the next two years, the company suffered losses though it purchased Broderbund for $400 million. In 1999 TLC was then sold to Matter for $4.2 billion. This sale was dumped the most disastrous acquisitions in recent history because Mattel dint makes any profits instead made losses that resulted in a loss of $3 billion of shareholders value. The loss was terrible, and Mattel sued O’Leary for misleading them, but he disputed the charges.

He then invested in StorageNow Holdings for $500k and sold his shares later for $4.5 million. Kevin has also established other projects among them is O’Leary Funds which has over $1 billion capital, O’Leary Ventures, and ETF and investing.

Apart from the business, he has also been making appearances on media. Some of these appearances have been on Dragons’ Den and Shark Tank, Discovery Channel’s Discovery Project Earth, The Lang and O’Leary Exchange on CBS, and Redemption Inc.
