Dan McNeil Biography

Dan McNeil is a popular American radio host who is better known for being the former co-host of the Chicago sports radio station “670, The Score (WSCR)” alongside Danny Parkins. For more than 25 years, he also worked at a Chicago radio station. He is active on social media with over 33,000 followers on his Twitter: @DannyMac670.

McNeil got his major breakthrough in his career as a radio host in 1992. He joined the Chicago sports radio station “670 The Score” in 1992. He co-hosted the show with Danny Parkins for almost 28 years. WSCR-the branded 670 The Score, is a commercial sports radio station that first broadcast on April 12, 1922, and is licensed to serve Chicago.

Previously, McNeil worked at Hubbard Radio classic rock station “WDRV FM 97.1”. He has also worked at various radio stations as a salesman and as a radio personality. Presently, he runs his own radio show, “The Mo’Kelly Show,” which airs on KFI AM640 on iHeartRadio.

Regarding his education, he attended The University of Texas, where he received a Bachelor’s degree in mass communication.
