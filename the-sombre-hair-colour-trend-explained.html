Embrace Effortless Elegance With The Sombr Hair Colour Trend

Posted by Billy Koelling on Saturday, August 31, 2024

As we bid farewell to the old and usher in the new year, many of us find ourselves craving change, and what better way to refresh your look than with a subtle yet striking hair trend? Say hello to ‘sombré’, a fusion of ‘soft’ and ‘ombré’, offering a more natural and blended take on the traditional ombré style. In 2024, sombré is set to dominate the hair colour scene, providing a low-maintenance option for those seeking a change without the drastic commitment.

What Is The Sombré Hair Colour All About?

Sombré, a soft ombré, is all about achieving a gradual transition of colour from the roots to the tips, creating dimension and depth while maintaining a natural appearance. Unlike its predecessor, ombré, which showcased a distinct contrast between dark roots and light ends, sombré opts for a more diffused and subtle gradient.

Versatility Across Hair Colours And Textures

One of the standout features of the sombré trend is its universal appeal. Suitable for all hair colours, types and textures, sombré works by enhancing your natural base colour, mimicking the way the sun naturally lightens hair. Whether you’re a blonde, brunette or somewhere in between, sombré can effortlessly elevate your look.

How To Achieve The Sombré Look

To rock the sombré trend, leave your roots as their natural colour or opt for a base colour close to your natural shade. The key lies in adding soft, subtle highlights that gradually lighten towards the ends. For those with darker hair, a gloss application can help balance the colour, ensuring a seamless blend without harsh contrasts. Remember to bring a reference photo to guide your colourist and communicate your desired outcome effectively.

Benefits Of The Sombré Do

Sombré hair isn’t just about aesthetics; it also promotes the health of your mane. By minimising the need for frequent lightening, sombré is an excellent choice for those looking to reduce expensive salon visits. This low-maintenance style can last up to a year, thanks to its soft gradient that conceals any demarcation lines.

Caring For Your Sombré Hair

While sombré is known for being long-lasting and low-maintenance, a little aftercare goes a long way. Regular toning every eight weeks helps maintain brightness, and a 10-minute clear gloss prior to a haircut or blowout adds a healthy shine. Opt for sulphate-free shampoo and indulge in a hydrating mask weekly to keep your colour vibrant and your hair nourished.

Caution Against DIY

While we adore DIY solutions, experimenting with lighteners at home for sombré is a big no-no. Leave the colouring to the professionals to avoid potential mishaps and ensure a flawless result.

The Bottom Line

The sombré hair trend emerges as the go-to choice for those seeking a natural, lived-in look. Its adaptability, low maintenance and universal appeal make this hair colour a look that’s here to stay. So, if you’re looking to switch up your style this year, sombré might just be the transformative trend you’ve been waiting for in 2024.
