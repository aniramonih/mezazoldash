Early Life & Biography

Jose Feliciano was born on 10th September 1945 in Lares Puerto Rico. He was born blind, is the fourth born in his family out of 11 children in his family. He began involving in music at the age of 3 as his uncle used to play cuatro. His family later moved to Spanish Harlem in New York at the age of 5, and when he was seven years, he was playing the accordion. His father also bought him a guitar and taught himself how to play it.

He attended The Light House School for the blind and had a guitar lesson with Harold Morris. He dropped out when he was at high school in order to help his family and would go play in coffee houses and clubs upon invitation

Personal Life

Career

He released his first single in 1964 entitled Everybody, Do the Click. His first album came in 1965 and 1966 called The Voice and Guitar of Jose Feliciano. He released several other albums, which made the RCA executives ask him to write a song for them in Spanish. He also got a chance to perform on Pirate Radio stations.

He was introduced to RCA producer, Rick Jarrard and together, they released an album, Feliciano. He recorded several other albums with Rick Jarrard. Feliciano continued to receive more invitations to perform as he continued to release more songs. In 1970 he released another album consisting of Christmas music. He has also worked with other musicians to release several songs.

Awards & Achievements

Feliciano has received several awards and titles from his career. He won two Grammy Awards in 1968 is the best new artist and also the best pop performer of the year.
