Kevin Spacey Career

Kevin Spacey Career – In a 1981 production of Henry VI, Part 1 at the New York Shakespeare Festival, Spacey made his debut on stage in a professional capacity as a spear bearer. He made his Broadway debut the following year in the lead role of Oswald in the Kennedy Center's Eisenhower Theatre production of Henrik Ibsen's Ghosts, which starred Liv Ullmann and was directed by John Neville. The Misanthrope by Molière followed, in which he played Philinte. Spacey made an appearance in a performance of Hurlyburly by David Rabe in 1984, playing all of the male roles (he would later portray Mickey in the movie adaptation). The Seagull by Anton Chekhov with Colleen Dewhurst and David Strathairn was the following. In 1986, Spacey made an appearance in a New Jersey dinner theater's rendition of Sleuth.

Spacey first gained notoriety on the stage in 1986 when Jonathan Miller's acclaimed production of Eugene O'Neill's Long Day's Journey into Night starred him as Jamie, the oldest Tyrone son, alongside Jack Lemmon, Peter Gallagher, and Bethel Leslie.
