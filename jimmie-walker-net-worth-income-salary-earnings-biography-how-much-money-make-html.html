Jimmie Walker Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography, How much money make?

Posted by Patria Henriques on Saturday, August 24, 2024
Known for his role as James Evans Jr. (J.J.), the oldest son of Florida and James Evans Sr. on the CBS television series Good Times, which aired from 1974 to 1979, Walker was born James Carter “Jimmie” Walker on June 25, 1947, in Chicago, Illinois, and is an American actor and comedian. In addition to his appearance on the programme, Walker’s character was recognised for the catchphrase “Dy-no-mite!” which he also used in a television commercial for a Panasonic series of cassette and 8-track tape recorders that aired during the mid-1970s. He also appeared in the films Let’s Do It Again with Amos and The Greatest Thing That Almost Happened, both of which featured James Earl Jones, among others. Walker’s stand-up comedy performance is now on tour across the United States.
