Zelda Rubinstein Finance Overview

Early Life and Education of Zelda Rubinstein

Zelda Rubinstein was born in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, to Polish-Jewish immigrant parents. She was the youngest of three children. Despite being the only short-statured person in her family, she thrived academically.

Zelda earned a bachelor’s degree in bacteriology from the University of Pittsburgh. This strong educational foundation set her on a path that would later merge with her passion for acting.

Acting Career

Rubinstein’s journey into acting began at the age of 45. She studied acting at the University of California.

Her breakout role came in 1982 with the film Poltergeist, where she played the unforgettable psychic Tangina Barrons. This role earned her critical acclaim and opened doors to various other acting opportunities.

Rubinstein appeared in films like Sixteen Candles, Under the Rainbow, and Teen Witch. She also had notable TV roles in Picket Fences, where she played Ginny Weedon, and Scariest Places on Earth, where she served as the narrator.

Her voice-over work for Skittles‘ Taste the Rainbow campaign is another highlight of her diverse career.

Activism and Advocacy

Zelda was not just an actress but a passionate activist. She was an early and outspoken advocate for HIV/AIDS awareness, participating in campaigns aimed at promoting safer sex.

Her activism came at a personal cost, impacting her career, but Rubinstein remained dedicated. She was also a strong advocate for the rights of little people, using her platform to fight against societal prejudices and promote inclusion.
