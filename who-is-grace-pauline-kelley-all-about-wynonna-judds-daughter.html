Grace Pauline Kelley’s Mother and Grandmother Are Among The World’s Most Recognized Country Singers

Clearly, Grace Pauline Kelley was born into fame. Both her mother, Wynonna Judd, and grandmother, Naomi Judd are very famous for their work as country singers, although her mother, Wynonna is more recognized. Grace’s mother, Wynonna has established herself as one of the most renowned and decorated female artists in the country music genre. With an impressive track record, Wynonna has achieved 19 No. 1 singles throughout her career.

Wynonna first rose to prominence in the 1980s alongside her mother, Naomi, when they formed the beloved country music duo called The Judds. Together, they released seven albums under the Curb Records label and produced 26 singles, with an impressive 14 of them reaching No. 1 on the charts. However, after some time, The Judds disbanded when Naomi was diagnosed with hepatitis. Following this, Wynonna embarked on a solo career.

In her solo career, Wynonna has released eight studio albums, a live album, a holiday album, and two compilation albums, accompanied by over 20 singles. Her initial solo singles, “She Is His Only Need,” “I Saw the Light,” and “No One Else on Earth,” consecutively topped the U.S. country singles charts. In 1996, her fourth number-one and top-ten hit, “To Be Loved by You,” further solidified her success. Three of her albums have received platinum or higher certifications from the RIAA.

Wynonna has solidified her status as one of the best-selling country artists of all time. Her solo albums and singles bear her single name, Wynonna. In recognition of their exceptional contributions to country music, Wynonna and her mother, Naomi were honored in 2022 with induction into the prestigious Country Music Hall of Fame as The Judds.

Grace Pauline Kelley Has a Troubled History of Legal Woes and Prison Sentences
