Dena is portrayed by actress May Calamawy, who skillfully brings depth and complexity to the character.1

Character Dynamics and Struggles

In the series, Dena is depicted as a graduate student navigating the complexities of a conservative Muslim family in modern American society. Her character grapples with the restrictions imposed by overprotective parents, highlighting the double standards applied to her and her brother, Ramy. This theme of disparate treatment is central to Dena’s storyline.

Exploring Societal Issues

Dena’s journey in the series delves into broader societal issues, such as misogyny and cultural expectations. Interactions with her controversial Uncle Naseem complicate her struggle for independence and identity. One episode focuses specifically on the societal pressure Dena faces, prompting her to consider pursuing her desires over conforming to her parents’ expectations.

May Calamawy’s portrayal of Dena Hassan adds a critical layer to “Ramy,” exploring themes of gender, culture, and personal freedom within the context of a Muslim-American family. Her performance has received acclaim, contributing significantly to the show’s success.

Ramy Youssef: A Conversation on Faith, Family, and Hollywood Strikes

Early Warnings and Family Values

During a panel discussion on faith and comedy at the Just For Laughs festival on Saturday, Ramy Youssef shared insights into his father’s early advice, which foretold the ongoing Hollywood actors’ strike.4

Youssef, known for his portrayal of Muslim faith in the Hulu series “Ramy,” recounted his Egyptian-born father’s discouragement from pursuing a career in the arts, emphasizing the need to provide for the family in the future.

From Hobby to Reality

Reflecting on his father’s warning, Youssef expressed how the current industry shutdown, with Hollywood actors seeking fair pay and residuals from streamers, echoes the cautionary words. “Now we’re in the strike, and, well, here it is! The moment my father spoke of. Enjoy the last Montreal,” he remarked to the ComedyPRO conference audience.

Personal Journeys in Comedy

Comedian panels at Just For Laughs often provide a therapeutic atmosphere, and Youssef’s discussion with fellow creator Jasmeet Singh Raina was no exception.

They delved into the personal aspects of their respective journeys, with Raina preparing to launch his Canadian comedy, “Late Bloomer,” centered around a YouTube content creator balancing newfound internet fame and dating within his devout Sikh family.

Ramy: Balancing Faith and Fame

Youssef, the creator, producer, director, and star of Hulu’s “Ramy,” explored his role as a devout Muslim navigating the complexities of a politically divided New Jersey neighborhood. The show earned him a Golden Globe in 2020, along with two Emmy nominations and a Peabody Award.

Faith and Fame: A Personal Struggle

Youssef opened up about the internal conflict he faced while portraying his Muslim faith on screen. He shared moments of guilt and shame, questioning the impact on his Muslim American community and fearing possible rejection after the show’s debut. Despite his concerns, he found unexpected support, even from members of his local mosque.

Brand Identity in Comedy

The discussion also touched on the unique aspects of Youssef and Raina’s comedic brands. Youssef, with his portrayal of Islam in “Ramy,” grappled with the juxtaposition of personal solace and sitcom success. Raina, recognized for wearing a turban as a member of the Sikh faith, discussed how it became an integral part of his brand, shaping his character in the upcoming Crave comedy.

Beyond TV: Ventures in Film

Closing the conversation, Youssef shared details about his upcoming venture in the film “Poor Things,” where he will appear alongside Emma Stone, Willem Dafoe, and Mark Ruffalo. The feature film, directed by Yorgos Lanthimos, adds another dimension to Youssef’s diverse career in the entertainment industry.
