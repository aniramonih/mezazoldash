Katrina Lake is a famous businesswoman and the founder and former CEO of  Stitch Fix who has a net worth of $200 million dollars. Katrina Lake has a lot of experience working in the fashion and beauty industry.

Many businesswomen are rising in their careers every day. Every year, Forbes takes out the list of millionaires and billionaires whose businesses have earned the highest revenue since its establishment. 

After the COVID-19 pandemic, many businesses faced a drop in their income as well as their stock price. Stitch Fix was also one of them. But even after everything, Lake is still listed as one of the growing millionaires in America. 

Stitch Fix is a brand that focuses on establishing and developing different people's styles without hassle. It is working toward people who are confused about their style preferences. The brand operates all over the United States and in different parts of the United Kingdom.

Due to these experiences, Forbes considered Katrina Lake one of the wealthiest self-made women in America. 

What Is Katrina Lake Net Worth In 2022?

Katrina Lake is an American businesswoman who has a net worth of around $200 million dollars. 

Katrina is the founder and former CEO of the fashion and clothing brand Stitch Fix. She was also the CEO of Stitch Fix, but she stepped out of the position in April 2021 and remained as one of the executive chairpersons of the board. 

Now, most of Katrina's income comes from the brand she owns. 

Stitch Fix is a fashion brand for both men and women which does not only sells different fashion items but also helps in the development of one's style as time increases. 

Stitch Fix was founded by Katrina Lake back in October 2010. Her sole motive was to blend personal style and fashion with clothing brands. Back in 2011, while she was studying at Harvard, she got her first order. Now the company works nationwide by serving people of all choices. It was established with a focus on women's fashion, but since 2016, they upgraded to men's fashion.

Finally, in 2017, Stitch Fix expanded its women's clothing to plus size. 

Now, the clothing brand has expanded its section, including kids and maternity sections. 

Stitch Fix works with 1000+ brands like Adidas, Nike, Columbia, and so on, where one can choose any brands of their choice and style themselves. One great thing about Stitch Fix is that they work with different stylists who are their team members. 

When Katrina was 34, Stitch Fix became a Public Company, and at that time, she became the youngest CEO to lead an IPO of the company. Last year around January, Stitch Fix's stock price increased to $96.92 per share. That was the time when Katrina Lake became a billionaire. 

However, the growth didn't sustain for a long time. The pandemic affected the company's revenue badly. The company's stock was reduced by 92%, and so was Katrina's worth. Due to this, the company's warehouses were temporarily closed. 

The company mentioned that it had a problem attracting new customers, so its annual revenue might be affected in the current year, 2022. 

Despite the drop in the company's worth, Katrina Lake was still listed in Forbe's List of Richest Self-Made Women. The company is planning to step up its business slowly despite the loss it faced.

Considering all the earnings as a founder of Stitch Fix and other investments, Katrina Lake has almost a worth of $1.1 billion.

Katrina Lake Career Earnings

Katrina Lake is one of the richest self-made women in the United States as per Forbes annual data. 

Katrina was born in San Francisco, California on December 24, 1982. She has a mixed ethnicity as her mother was a Japanese immigrant who used to teach the Japanese language at different public schools. Her dad, on the other hand, was a doctor at two different universities, the University of Minnesota and the University of California, San Francisco. 

While growing up, she wanted to follow in her father's footsteps, and she even got enrolled in a pre-med course at Stanford University. However, her interest in the field didn't last long. She later on, became interested in business and economics and graduated from Stanford in 2005. 

She joined The Parthenon Group in 2005. There, she realized that companies lacked the capacity to utilize their data properly, which helped them understand their customers properly. There, she worked in Marketing and Blogging Outreach for almost 2 years.

After The Parthenon Group, she worked at Leader Ventures as an associate. After completing her undergraduate degree, she also worked at Polyvore as a Marketing and Blogging Outreach. 

She started her graduate degree in MBA at the Harvard Business School in 2009, and that was when the idea of starting Stitch Fix came to her mind. The inspiration came from Trunk Club working toward men's styling and fashion.

She took a survey among different people to know about their fashion choices and preferences.  Along with her classmate, Erin Morrison Flynn, they started to buy different inventories for the people who submitted their style preferences. 

Katrina even had access to proper data and marketing skills to grow her business. She completed her MBA in 2011. 

Other than Stitch Fix, Katrina Lake is also a director and member of the boards of two companies, Grubhub and Glossier. 

She was listed in Fortune Magazines Under 40 in 2016 under 100 Most Creative People in Business. Similarly, she was considered Disruptor of the Year by Retail Dive. 

In 2020, in collaboration with Lori Greiner, Katina Lake appeared as a guest shark at Shark Tank to build children's eyewear collections. 

Katrina Lake married John Clifford, an investor, and the couple has two sons.
